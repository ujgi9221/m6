## Who Actually Needs Windows Hosting?

Most people default to Linux hosting. It's cheaper to operate, most PHP/WordPress tutorials assume Linux, and the major cPanel hosts are Linux-based. That's fine — for a lot of use cases, Linux is perfectly good.

But there's a whole crowd of developers and businesses for whom Windows hosting isn't optional:

- You're building in **ASP.NET or ASP.NET Core**
- You need **Microsoft SQL Server** (not just MySQL)
- Your stack involves **IIS**, **Node.js on Windows**, or legacy **Classic ASP**
- You want to mix **PHP + .NET + SQL Server** in the same environment
- You're a **web designer or agency** who resells hosting to clients and needs full Plesk control per site

For this crowd, M6.Net is one of the few providers that's genuinely specialized — not just offering Windows as an afterthought.

## A Few Things to Know Before You Sign Up

No provider is perfect, and M6.Net has some real tradeoffs worth knowing about:

- **No monthly billing on shared plans** — you're committing to at least a year. The annual price is low enough that this isn't a dealbreaker, but it's worth noting.
- **Domain registration and SSL are separate on lower-tier plans** — the free domain only comes with reseller plans (prepaid yearly/2-year). On shared plans, you'll need to bring your own domain or pay separately.
- **Pre-sales support can be slow** — multiple reviewers have noted that technical support through the customer portal is solid, but email inquiries before you're a customer can take a day or more. If you have detailed pre-sales questions, use the ticket system once you sign up rather than the general contact form.
- **Mixed user reviews exist** — like most hosts operating at this price point, reviews on third-party sites are varied. Long-term customers (some since 2012) tend to rate them highly; a smaller set of users have reported billing confusion or downtime issues. Worth keeping the 30-day guarantee in mind.
